Let's Hear It

So, this happened to me recently.



A malfunctioned external hard disk with no backup, a decade's worth of lost memories & an immediate panic attack.

Problem is I did not back up on the cloud.

And this experience completely changed my habits.

My Photo Journey Now


Of course, backup did not exist until this painful experience.

But Not Everyone Cares



My user research indicates that users typically fall in one of the four quadrants based on their behavior of photo viewing and backup.

The Pragmatics are organized but only look at photos when they have a specific objective in mind (i.e. making a scrapbook) where as the Paranoid is really on top of the backup and organization process. In contrast to the Queen of Instagram, Instagram is like a daily diary which almost serves as a "backup" for her. Finally, the Indifferent just couldn't careless about organizing photos never mind backing up.

Now The Problem



Backing up photos is not a developed habit at all.

And for those who backup, they don't quite understand how Google Photos work.

Here's What I Think



Habit building is a sequential process and it's about being in the right time, space and context. A great place to start would be Google's own platforms.
