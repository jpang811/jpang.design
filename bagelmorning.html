Morning Bagels, Coffee & Innovation

A dose of creative thinking and team building.

Bagels for Thought

Inspired by my Service Jam win, my colleague and I decided to introduce a similar intiative in the workplace to encourage creative ideas, innovation and collaboration at work. We called it “Bagel Morning”, which involves a sumptuous bagel, its cream cheese culprit and coffee.

Testing out Bagel Morning

For our first test, we launched the first Bagel Morning amongst a few colleagues we collaborate with on a regular basis including design leads, project managers, designers and a customer experience specialist.

Based on our existing collaborations, we decided to address design principles as a first topic. At that moment in time, our shared pain point is in the different processes and protocols when launching features for projects, from the mobile app to redesigning the desktop experience. As our products evolved, we needed a set of standards and principles to guide us and fall back on.

We started by looking at examples of other design practices such as Airbnb on "Belong Anywhere", Microsoft's inclusive design and Google's material design. Subsequently, we discussed our pain points and used those as pointers for agreeing on the principles that are most relevant for us and our work.

At the end of the session, we walked away with six key design principles. As a first test, the Bagel Morning workshop generated very positive feedback and they actually wanted to have these workshops more often.

How about Storytelling?

The fun part about designing workshops is that I get to learn too. I've always had a knack for storytelling through my work but deducing them to theories and techniques was not something I thought about consciously. We looked through different academic materials and even enrolled in Pixar's storytelling course to get a sense of how to structure the course.

Storytelling has many contexts. It can be for entertainment, business pitches, user engagement or simply just recounting a recent trip in Iceland.

Innovation is One Step at a Time

The thing about innovation is it doesn't have to be inventing a completely new product or experience. Sometimes, simplifying or improving the core part of it is already innovation in and of itself.

The best part is everyone's got it in them. It's just a matter of taking gradual steps and a mindset that needs to be nurtured.

As with all designs, even workshops need iteration.
